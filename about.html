Who I Am

Building Africa’s digital future from the inside.

RA

I am Regan Odhiambo Ayiecho, born and raised in Kisumu, Kenya. I am a Mastercard Foundation Scholar pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Software Engineering at the African Leadership University in Kigali, Rwanda.

My journey into technology was not a straight line. I taught Mathematics at St. Joseph Ng’ula Primary School, mentored hundreds of students through scholarship applications at Eaal Foundation, and then built a pan-African platform from zero budget. Every step taught me something different about people, problems and persistence.

Today I am co-founder and CTO of Young Africans Network — a platform serving members across 12 African countries built on GitHub Pages and Firebase with no hosting cost. I am also building AfriMeet, Africa’s own video conferencing platform designed to replace Zoom and Google Meet for the continent’s 500,000+ NGOs, universities and organizations.

I believe Africa should own its digital infrastructure. My goal is to build technology that keeps African money, data and value on the continent.
